PHISHING TACTICS: THE THREAT MODEL

Phishing exploits human trust to breach digital security. It is the primary vector for data theft, often camouflaged as communications from a **trusted source** (e.g., banks, IT support, supervisors).

Key attack vectors often involve urgency, fear, or promised financial gain:

  • **Spear Phishing:** Targeting specific individuals with highly personalized, convincing messages.
  • **Vishing:** Phishing conducted over voice calls.
  • **Smishing:** Phishing conducted via SMS/text messages.

RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

  • **URL Mismatch:** Link destination does not match the displayed link text.
  • **Sense of Urgency:** Immediate action required (e.g., "Account suspended! Click to restore.").
  • **Suspicious Sender:** Email domain does not match the organization's official domain.

TACTICAL DEFENSES

  • **Hover, Don't Click:** Always inspect the link destination status bar before interaction.
  • **MFA Activation:** Enable Multi-Factor Authentication on all critical accounts.
  • **Verify Out-of-Band:** If suspicious, contact the company via a trusted, known phone number, not the one provided in the message.
